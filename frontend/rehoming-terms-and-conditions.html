Rehoming Terms and Conditions
Rehoming Terms and Conditions


These are the terms and conditions we ask each rehomer to agree to before rehoming. You will be prompted to read these when applying to rehome.

Our Rights

  1. We retain the right to visit the equine at any time after rehoming, with or without notice
  2. We retain the right to seize the equine without notice where these terms and conditions have been breached or we believe it is in the best interests of the equine to do so.


The Rehomer's Responsibilities

  1. If the rehomer wants to change the location where the equine is kept, they shall give us at least two weeks notice unless there are significant extenuating circumstances.
  2. The rehomer is not permitted to take the equine out of the UK without written permission.
  3. The rehomer is responsible for all costs relating to the equine during the duration of the loan, including the cost of returning it to us if required.
  4. The rehomer shall indemnify Leigh Rescue Centre and its servants against any claim for injury or damage caused by the equine while on loan to them.
  5. In agreeing to rehome the equine, the rehomer has taken into consideration and problems the equine may have as listed on the listing and contract
  6. If the equine is returned to or seized by Leigh Rescue Centre, all paperwork related to it will be returned by the rehomer.
  7. If the equine is returned to or seized by Leigh Rescue Centre, the rehomer will no longer have any contact with it.
  8. Except in an emergency, at least two weeks notice should be given if the rehomer wishes to return the equine.


Care of the Equine

  1. The equine is not rehomed with any tack or equipment, unless specifically agreed otherwise.
  2. While we request that the equine be only used for the agreed for purpose, we do not warranty its fitness for that purpose in anyway.
  3. The rehomer will provide the equine with suitable veterinary care whenever it is in ill-health or injured and will follow the vet's advice. The rehomer bears sole responsibility for resulting fees.
  4. The rehomer must follow an appropriate worming programme, ensure that the equine's feet are regularly trimmed and/or shod by a qualified farrier, and keep the equine's vaccinations for ‘flu and tetanus up to date.
  5. The equine remains our property throughout the loan.
  6. If a vet recommends euthanasia, or in an emergency, the equine shall be humanely destroyed either by a veterinary surgeon or a licensed knacker. The rehomer must notify Leigh Rescue Centre as soon as possible.
  7. The equine must not be used for breeding.
  8. The equine must not sold, given away or used commercially without written permission from Leigh Rescue centre.
  9. If the equine is a colt too young for castration, the rehomer agrees to have him castrated as soon as possible.

Who We are

Leigh Rescue Centre is a not for profit, family run project. Our aim is to give people, ponies and places a second chance. We are nothing special, posh or flush and our set up is still very basic, but we are constantly striving to provide better facilities for the ponies, visitors and volunteers.



What We Do

We rescue, care for and rehome ponies and horses. We usually have about 10 - 20 ponies/horses at any time- each with their own unique story. Neglected, orphaned, abused, sick, injured,and/or abandoned. All our horses are rescues. Some have been signed over from the police or R.S.P.C.A, some are cast offs from dealers, and some have been brought in by loving owners no longer able to look after them.

With the help of the community we do our best to look after them and lovingly turn them around. The horses are regularly seen by the vet, farrier, and equine dentist. We also look to serve the community in what little kind ways we safely can and encourage others to do likewise - be it visiting a care home with a pony; helping the homeless, or providing families with food, clothes bikes,and books, making shoe boxes, collecting clothes for Romania etc A little love goes a long way....



How We Do It

We initially set the project up with a large settlement and we still are mostly family funded. Donations only making up a fraction of our weekly expenses, but it all helps and a careful note is made. Just recently a lovely couple provided us with a year's supply of hay, a building site provided pallets, another supplied equine medical supplies, another cold drinks etc all of which have been much appreciated and really cheers us. Donations towards, vet, farrier, horse dentist, fencing, shelters, and rent, are a great help, but please, please don't feel you have to. We're here to serve the community, not to be served.



Why We Do It

We aim to help some of the many neglected and unwanted horses in existence around the UK while benefiting the community as best we can through opportunities for people to get involved. We believe that turning a needy pony or place around can help turn people around too - it's a two way process. In particular, we have found that people with autism often form an amazing bond with rescues, and as a result A considerable proportion of the ponies that have come through the rescue centre have been lovingly re-homed.with families affected by autism.

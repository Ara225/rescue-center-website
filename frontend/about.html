OUR AIM

To give people, ponies and places a second chance



Who We are

Leigh Rescue Centre is a not for profit, family run charity, rescuing, rehabilitating and rehoming horses/ponies in and with the community. We normally work with the NHS, and local authorities as well as people/families and businesses in the community. Rescues, like people have different personalities and can have physical, emotional, and behavioral issues. People often see themselves in a horse, and when they turn that horse around, they find the horse turns them around.

The charity was founded in 2016 and is based in Greater Manchester and Cheshire. We are not currently a registered charity with professional fund raisers, high administration and paid staff. We do it out of love for people and ponies mostly out of our own money. Present yearly donations come nowhere near the charity registration fee, though that may be an option in the feature. Any money donated is noted and goes 100% on the rescues unless requested otherwise.

What We Do

We rescue, care for and rehome ponies and horses. We additionally try to make a difference to the people and places around us - be it visiting a care home with a pony; helping the homeless, or providing families with food, clothes bikes, and books, making shoe boxes, collecting clothes for Romania etc A little love goes a long way....

We usually have about 10 - 20 ponies/horses at any time- each with their own unique story. Neglected, orphaned, abused, sick, injured,and/or abandoned. All our horses are rescues. Some have been signed over from the police or R.S.P.C.A, some are cast offs from dealers, and some have been brought in by loving owners no longer able to look after them. With the help of the community we do our best to look after them and lovingly turn them around. The horses are regularly seen by the vet, farrier, and equine dentist.

How We Do It

We initially set the project up with a large settlement and we still are mostly family funded. Donations only make up a fraction of our weekly expenses, but it all helps and a careful note is made. The horses we have come from those offered to us by owners, police etc. We do most of the work ourselves and rent farms or land to keep the horses on.

Volunteers are welcome (though we currently can't recruit any due to COVID); we always need help with a wide range of things from building shelters to taming horses. We also have a selection of horses available for rehoming. Donations towards vet, farrier, horse dentist, fencing, shelters, and rent, are a great help, but please, please don't feel you have to. We're here to serve the community, not to be served.

Why We Do It

We aim to help some of the many neglected and unwanted horses and ponies in existence around the UK while benefiting the community as best we can through opportunities for people to get involved and community outreaches.

We believe that turning a needy pony or place around can help turn people around too - it's a two way process. In particular, we have found that people with autism often form an amazing bond with rescues, and as a result A considerable proportion of the ponies that have come through the rescue centre have been lovingly re-homed with families affected by autism.
